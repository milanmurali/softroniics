J. Robert Oppenheimer

About the Legend

  • Born on April 22, 1904, in New York City, USA.
  • A brilliant physicist known as the "father of the atomic bomb."
  • Directed the Manhattan Project, the U.S. research project that developed the first atomic bomb during World War II.
  • Played a crucial role in the design and testing of the bomb, including the Trinity test in July 1945.
  • His leadership and contributions led to the successful detonation of the atomic bombs dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki in August 1945, hastening the end of World War II.
  • Oppenheimer died on February 18, 1967, leaving behind a complex legacy as both a scientific genius and a figure embroiled in the moral dilemmas of the nuclear age.
